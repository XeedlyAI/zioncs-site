## Target Audience / Buyer Personas

The user identified four buyer segments. Each becomes a persona with full development in Phase 1 (next session). Initial sketches:

### Persona 1: Residential Property Owner — Repair & Upgrade
- **Trigger:** existing concrete failing (cracks, settling), or upgrading driveway / patio / pool deck for resale or quality of life
- **Pain points:** unreliable contractors who don't show, delays, mess left behind, surprise costs
- **Buying signals:** searches "[service] near me", "concrete contractor [city] Utah", reads Google reviews carefully
- **Conversion path:** Get a Quote form OR Book Discovery Call
- **Silo:** RESIDENTIAL

### Persona 2: Builder / GC needing Reliable Sub
- **Trigger:** spec home build, multi-unit residential, or remodel needing flatwork
- **Pain points:** subs who flake on schedule, quality issues that cost the GC reputation, billing disputes
- **Buying signals:** reaches out via referral, asks about commercial/builder pricing, wants to see project history
- **Conversion path:** Discovery Call (relationship-driven)
- **Silo:** BUILDER

### Persona 3: Commercial Developer
- **Trigger:** new commercial site (retail, multifamily, industrial) or property maintenance contract
- **Pain points:** scale + scheduling reliability, insurance/bonding requirements, ability to handle large pours
- **Buying signals:** RFP/bid process, asks about crew size and equipment, wants references at similar scale
- **Conversion path:** Discovery Call → formal proposal
- **Silo:** COMMERCIAL

### Persona 4: Enterprise / Multi-Site Operator (e.g., Waste/Disposal Cos)
- **Trigger:** dozens of trash pads / dumpster enclosures across portfolio needing build + ongoing maintenance
- **Pain points:** finding a single contractor who can service multiple sites consistently, reliable cadence, predictable invoicing
- **Buying signals:** procurement-driven, RFP, multi-year contract conversation
- **Conversion path:** Discovery Call → enterprise-tier proposal
- **Silo:** ENTERPRISE

**Status:** these are first-pass sketches. Phase 1 follow-up: run `skills/BUYER-PERSONA.md` to develop each into full personas with goals, pain points, triggers, objections, messaging strategy.

## Design Direction

User-provided inspiration sites (these are the *aesthetic* references, not competitors):

1. **vcass.vic.edu.au** — Victorian College of the Arts, sophisticated/artsy
2. **chiselindustries.com** — chisel-themed; high-end design feel
3. **happyrobot.ai** — AI/tech with personality

**Translation: the client wants to break out of typical concrete-contractor aesthetics.** Most concrete sites are photo-heavy, hard-hat-centric, conservative layouts. The inspiration trio points toward:
- Sophisticated typography
- Restrained, intentional photography (not stock-style worker portraits)
- Brand-forward design — the company as a *brand*, not just a service
- Modern interaction patterns (motion, scroll-driven content, possibly minimal)

This is a significant signal. The rebuild should NOT replicate the existing site's visual language.

### Tone

- Operator-grade and craft-focused (matches the contractor reality)
- Confident without bluster; specific differentiators over generic "best in class"
- Lean on the values vocabulary already established: integrity, hard work, quality, pride, honest, lasting
- Avoid: SaaS speak, AI/tech jargon (they're a contractor), folksy clichés ("hard hat heroes", "concrete pros")

### Photography direction (DECIDED)

- **Move away from** the existing site's "strong worker in hard hat folding arms" theme. The current site overdoes the worker-as-mascot tied to the logo likeness.
- **Move toward** project-craft photography: the *work*, the *result*, the *scale of jobs*. Show finished driveways, sport courts in use, dumpster-pad rows at a commercial site, a sport-court being striped, a sport-court being played on. The proof is the work.
- One or two team photos for the About page — keep it human — but not as the dominant visual treatment.

### Logo

- **Non-negotiable: keep existing logo.** Tiger-styled emblem + "ZION CONCRETE SPECIALISTS" wordmark.
- Asset is in `_inbox/webflow-assets/`. Extract during Phase 0 → Phase 1 transition.

### Brand colors

- Existing palette: bright orange + dark navy + light gray
- **Status: open for refinement.** Phase 3 (design calibration) will set the final palette. Candidate refinements:
  - Keep orange as the primary CTA color (proven working color, matches library's `#F97316` Pando primary)
